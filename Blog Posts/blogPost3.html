Here are the five most common website design mistakes that kill conversions, and how you can fix them.

1. Slow Loading Speed

The problem: If your site takes more than 3 seconds to load, most visitors leave before they even see your content. A slow site is like a locked storefront door.

The fix:

  • Compress images without losing quality
  • Use modern hosting platforms like Webflow or optimized WordPress
  • Minimize unnecessary plugins and scripts

At LMB Marketing, we build all websites with speed in mind so visitors stay engaged from the first click.

2. Confusing Navigation

The problem: If people cannot find what they are looking for, they leave. A cluttered menu or unclear structure frustrates visitors and stops them from taking action.

The fix:

  • Keep your navigation simple with clear categories
  • Limit menu options to the essentials
  • Always include a clear “Contact” or “Book a Call” button

Our approach is to build intuitive flows that guide users step by step toward conversion.

3. Weak or Missing Calls to Action

The problem: A beautiful website without clear CTAs is like a salesperson who never asks for the sale. Visitors do not know what to do next.

The fix:

  • Use strong, action-oriented CTAs like “Get Started,” “Book a Call,” or “Claim Your Free Website”
  • Place CTAs strategically throughout the page, not just at the bottom
  • Make buttons stand out with brand colors

We ensure every website we design has high impact CTAs that drive results.

4. Poor Mobile Experience

The problem: More than half of all web traffic is mobile. If your site does not work well on phones, you are losing customers daily.

The fix:

  • Use responsive design so the site adapts to every screen size
  • Make sure text is readable without zooming
  • Test buttons and forms to ensure they are easy to tap

Every site we create at LMB Marketing is mobile first because that is where your audience lives.

5. Lack of Trust Signals

The problem: Even if your site looks nice, visitors may not trust it enough to buy or submit their information. Missing testimonials, security icons, or case studies can make you look unproven.

The fix:

  • Add testimonials or reviews from real clients
  • Include recognizable trust badges or media features
  • Use case studies or portfolio examples to show results

At LMB Marketing, we integrate trust signals into every site we build so that visitors feel confident taking the next step.
