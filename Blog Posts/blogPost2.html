The 7 Metrics That Actually Matter in Paid Media Campaigns

To understand if your campaigns are profitable, sustainable, and scalable, you only need to focus on seven core metrics. These will tell you whether your marketing spend is moving your business forward or just burning budget.

The 7 Metrics That Actually Matter in Paid Media Campaigns

When you are running Google Ads or Meta Ads, the data can feel overwhelming. There are dozens of numbers inside each dashboard such as impressions, reach, cost per click, view-throughs, and more. The truth is that most of those metrics do not really matter when it comes to driving growth.

At LMB Marketing, we have managed paid campaigns across industries and budgets. What we have seen again and again is that the businesses who focus on the right numbers scale faster, waste less, and hit their revenue targets consistently.

1. Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)

ROAS tells you how much revenue you earn for every dollar spent on ads. This is the clearest measure of profitability. If you are spending $1 and making $4 back, that is a healthy ROAS.

On Google, ROAS is often higher for bottom of funnel keywords with strong intent. On Meta, ROAS performs well when campaigns use a mix of prospecting and retargeting to create a full funnel.

2. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

CAC measures how much it costs to acquire a new customer, factoring in your ad spend and sometimes other sales costs. It helps you understand if your business model is scalable. If your CAC is $200 and your average customer only brings in $150 of revenue, you will lose money as you grow.

Google often delivers customers with higher intent which can bring CAC down. Meta usually requires nurturing through retargeting, so CAC can look higher at first but pays off with proper funnel design.

3. Cost Per Lead (CPL)

CPL is the amount you spend to generate a lead such as a form fill, call, or email signup. For service-based businesses and B2B, CPL is a key driver. A low CPL means you can generate more opportunities for sales teams at a manageable cost.

Google captures high intent leads who are searching for your service right now. Meta generates leads from cold audiences who may need warming through follow ups and nurture sequences.

4. Click Through Rate (CTR)

CTR is the percentage of people who click on your ad after seeing it. It shows if your ad copy, creative, and targeting are resonating. A high CTR means people find your ad relevant. A low CTR means you are wasting impressions.

On Google, CTR reflects keyword alignment with user searches. On Meta, CTR reflects whether your creative and offer are strong enough to stop the scroll.

5. Conversion Rate (CVR)

CVR is the percentage of clicks that turn into the action you want such as purchases, leads, bookings, or sign ups. You cannot just buy clicks. You need clicks to convert. CVR shows how effective your landing pages, funnels, and offers are at turning interest into action.

On Google, high CVR usually means your landing page matches search intent. On Meta, strong creative plus a seamless funnel can drive high CVR even from colder traffic.

6. Lifetime Value (LTV)

LTV is the total revenue a customer generates during their relationship with your brand. It tells you how much you can afford to spend to acquire a customer. If your LTV is $1,000, spending $200 to acquire a customer is sustainable. If your LTV is $50, that spend would destroy your margins.

Both platforms benefit from knowing your LTV. With strong LTV you can scale aggressively on Meta. On Google you can bid higher on competitive search terms with confidence.

7. Frequency

Frequency is the average number of times an individual user sees your ad. Too low and your audience will not remember you. Too high and people get annoyed, which leads to ad fatigue and wasted spend. Frequency connects directly to the need for testing and refreshing creative.

On Meta, frequency management is critical because users see your ads in their daily feeds. On Google Display and YouTube, frequency helps you balance exposure without overwhelming your audience.

Why These Metrics Matter Most

When you focus on these seven metrics — ROAS, CAC, CPL, CTR, CVR, LTV, and Frequency — you are looking at the numbers that connect ad spend to business growth.

Other metrics like impressions, reach, or CPM can provide context, but they do not answer the most important question: are your campaigns profitable and scalable?

By keeping your eye on these seven, you will have the clarity you need to make better decisions, optimize campaigns, and get more out of every dollar you spend.
